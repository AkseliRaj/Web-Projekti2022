The Baltic Sea

The Baltic Sea is the youngest sea of our planet which formed around 10 000 - 15 000 years ago. The catchment area of the sea includes 14 countries and over 85 million habitants.

It has an unique ecosystem but it's severely affected by threats such as biodiversity loss and climate change. There are also a lot of overfishing, elevated levels of contaminants and litter.

A potential for development of maritime economy is extraordinary for the region. It could develop further shipping, blue bioeconomy, tourism, and enviromental techonologies in the region.

The shallowest part of the Baltic is the continental shelf, from which rise the islands of the Danish archipelago. There the Lille Strait divides eastern Jutland from the island of Funen, which is itself separated from Zealand by the deeper Store Strait.

Marked seasonality is the principal feature of the Baltic climate. Winters are long and cold, and summers are short and comparatively warm. Mean temperatures range from about -10 °C over the Gulfs of Bothnia and Finland in midwinter to about 17 °C over southern parts of the Baltic in midsummer.

It may sound a bit strange but the Baltic does not show much influence of tidal currents. One possible reason for it is the flow of saltwater with a higher density and strong countercurrent. This flow is visible specifically into the North Sea.
